Ames

City Overview

Ames has small town charm with the heart of a large city. Home of Iowa State University, one of the top engineering, agriculture, and veterinary colleges in the United States. With a large thriving campus in the middle of the city it is no surprise that Ames offers cultural, educational, business, and entertainment amenities that would be found in a much bigger metro area.

In 1858 Iowa Agricultural College and Model Farm was founded. This would later become Iowa State. Ames was founded 6 years later in 1964 as a depot town for the Cedar Rapids and Missouri River Railroad. In 1869 Iowa State had its first open enrollment, the same year that Ames was incorporated (Dec. 20, 1869). In 1891 The Dinky was constructed to connect the then small town to the ever growing campus.

Region and Classification

Ames is located on the western edge of Story County. Iowa State records attendance in the 30,000s on a yearly basis which adds to the already 65,000 population. The city's area covers 24.27 square miles with a small lake on campus and 2 small rivers on its boarders. Ames is classified as an urban area surrounded by beautiful rural farmland.

Income and Economy

Ames is known for its robust and stable economy. Many large companies like 3M, Barilla, Ball, and Danfoss Power Solutions built large plants in the city's industrial district. Ames is also the home to the US Department of Agriculture's National Animal Disease Center and the US Department of Energy's Ames Laboratory.

With the average mortgage in Ames being $274,500 for a 3 bedroom and the average median household income of $54,339, living is affordable.

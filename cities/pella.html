Pella

City Overview

Pella is a quaint little city in Iowa with a thriving Dutch community. The city hosts and annual tulip festival in the spring that brings people from all over the US and boast the show case of over 80 different types of tulips with over 130,000 blooms.

The city was incorporated on 1855. Eight years after 800 dutch settlers came to the plot of land and started to build what is now called the city of Pella. Its was originally built as a refuge for the dutch who had be ostracized for there laxed religious believes.

Region and Classification

Pella is located in Marion county between the Des Moines river and Skunk river. With a population of 10,352 it is on the small size which puts it in the classification of suburban more than urban but it boast an urban art and cultural scene. The city had canals created in part of the downtown area to mimic the Netherlands homelands and boost tourism. The canals are drained in the winter but are majestic either way. Pella sits on 8.73 square miles.

(Source:

  • Wikipedia)

Income and Economy

Pella has a number of large manufacturing facilities residing inside city limits. Companies like Pella Corporation, 3M, Weiler Corporation, and Hormel Foods have facilities there. Pella is also a large agricultural spot with a good portion of its population working in or along side the agricultural field.

With the average mortgage in Pella being $302,200 for a 3 bedroom and the average median household income of $80,270, living is affordable.

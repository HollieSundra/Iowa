Cedar Rapids

City Overview

Cedar Rapids is a city know for its arts and culture. With over 17 different museums and art galleries to explore. The city has produce notable actors like Ashton Kutcher and Elijah Woods as well as the famous painter Grant Wood. Musical theater still has a big presence in Cedar Rapids with the iconic Paramount theater still hosting shows.

The city was incorporated on January 15, 1849 as Cedar Rapids. Named after the river it bordered. In the beginning it was more of a town with only 400 residents but quickly grew to 1,600 in 1856 when the city was reincorporated.

Region and Classification

Today Cedar Rapids boast a population of 126,326 and is the second largest city in Iowa. The Cedar Rapids metropolitan area which includes some small surrounding suburbs brings the population total to 256,324 and spans 3 counties. The city itself sit in Linn county but the metro area also touches Benton and Jones counties. The city has a total area of around 72.07 square miles. With a population this size it is no surprise that it is classified as an urban area.

Income and Economy

For Iowans it will come as no surprise that we have one of the largest corn producing cities in the world. That city of course is Cedar Rapids. With grain providing over 4,000 jobs directly with a median salary of 85,000 and indirectly about 8,000. It is easy to say that Cedar Rapids has a healthy economy. Not only is grain a big economy boaster but there are also Fortune 500 companies like Collins Aerospace and CRST that have based themselves in Cedar Rapids.

With the average mortgage in Cedar Rapids being $185,000 for a 3 bedroom and the average median household income of $63,170, living is affordable.

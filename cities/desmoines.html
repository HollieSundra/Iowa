Des Moines

City Overview

Des Moines is a capital city with a vibrant night life and a thriving art scene. With many attractions and parks Des Moines is a tourist destination not to miss.

The city was incorporated on September 22, 1851, as Fort Des Moines, which was later shortened to Des Moines in 1857. With a rich history, Des Moines has several museums and historical to view and learn about.

  • Wikipedia)

Region and Classification

This thriving metropolitan area spans six counties and sits on the junction of the Des Moines river and the Raccoon river. Polk, Warren, Guthrie, Dallas, Jasper, and Madison each hold a portion of the metro areas 709,466 population with 214,133 living within main city limits. Des Moines has a city area of 90.65 square miles. With such a large area of land and population Des Moines is classified as an urban area with several suburbs within its metro area.

Income and Economy

Des Moines is a center for financial and insurance services. Many insurance companies like Principal Financial Group, EMC Insurance Group, Fidelity and Guaranty Life, Allied Insurance, GuideOne Insurance, Wellmark Blue Cross Blue Shield of Iowa, FBL Financial Group, and American Republic Insurance Company have established their headquarters there. With all the insurance business Des Moines has been referred to as the "Hartford of the West" and "Insurance Capital".

With the average mortgage in Des Moines being $204,890 for a 3 bedroom and the average median household income of $62,378, living is afforable.

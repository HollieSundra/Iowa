Iowa City

City Overview

Iowa City is the home of the University of Iowa. The city has everything from diverse night life to rich cultural and art districts. Many historical sites to explore and numerous place to shop. All of this in one of the safest cities in Iowa.

Iowa City was the first capital of the state of Iowa. While the city was founded in 1839 it was not incorporated until April 6, 1853. The capital was moved in 1858 to Des Moines.

  • Wikipedia)

Region and Classification

Iowa City is located in Johnson county and is the largest city in the county. It boasts a population of 74,828 with the metro area that extends into Washington county bring the total to 171,000. With these numbers it is no wonder that Iowa City is classified as an urban area with many suburbs in the metro area. With 25.28 square miles of land inside city limits there is plenty of room for growth.

(Source:

  • Wikipedia)

Income and Economy

Iowa City is a hub for healthcare with the University of Iowa's medical college and their teaching hospital employing over 30,000. The city also has several comprehensive specialist clinics that bring in patients from all over the US. Forbes named Iowa City the third best small metro area in the US in 2004.

With the average mortgage in Iowa City being $242,000 for a 3 bedroom and the average median household income of $51,925, living is affordable.
